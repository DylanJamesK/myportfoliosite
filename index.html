Pesonal Brand

Designing a logo for myself originated in 2015 during my undergraduate degree studying industrial design. We were forced to utilize manual and low-tech equipment for our early design projects, so one of the very first assignments was to carve an eraser to create your own signature stamp.

Naturally, I went with the DK- trying hard not to look like a knock-off Donkey Kong! I may still own the original eraser stamp somwhere. What I like about this logo is its minimalist design and how it allows you to discover more over time, inspiring creativity from primitive shapes.

Seven years later, in 2022, I felt the need to revamp my personal brand to symbolically grow with me. I wanted to maintain some of the original features of my first logo, such as the semi-circle 'D' and the embedded letters.

In this interation, I went for an even sharper, more cohesive look with no soft-cornered borders. It includes rings with incremental radius sizing and plenty of opportunity to animate.

Container Inspection Training

As contractors with the British Standards Institution, my colleague Alireza Bahremand and I developed a virtual reality CTPAT 7-Point Container Inspection training.

After the VR training application, we looked to explore the potential for inspecting a digital twin of an environment generated completed by sensors. We were provided a large LiDAR scan of a distribution center taken by a drone and mobile sensors. Here is our experimentation with navigating and measuring a point cloud environment in VR.

NASA BIG Idea Competition

As a contestant in NASA's BIG Idea Challenge, our prompt was to "...identify methods of collecting soil data from permanently shaded regions (PSRs) of the Moon." The speculation was that we could identify signs of water on our Moon in the form of crytalline ice. The reason this is quite difficult is because PSR craters are never exposed to light which results in them experiencing temperatures too cold for most electronics.

My team's solution was to design a disposable probe that could be launched from a lander into the PSR and relay information back. In order to share our vision, I developed an interactive VR experience that explains each step of our proposal by letting the user walk through it on the surface of the moon.

By the end of the competition, my team and I were chosen as finalists and won an award for 'Most Innovative Communication Strategy'- largely because of our VR experience I am proud to say.

Starbucks Zen Garden

In collaboration with Starbucks' Costa Rican coffee bean facility, I developed a small, soothing minigame to appear inside of a matterport link. In person, the site typically invites guests to take part in the coffee bean roasting process. The step you see below is where they rake the coffee beans to remove the shells. During to COVID-19, they wanted to find a way to provide a similar relaxing experience while completely remote.
